What Is ARIA?

WAI-ARIA is short for Web Accessibility Initiative - Accessible Rich Internet Applications. It is a set of attributes that can be added to HTML with the intent of improving accessibility among users who may have difficulties with viewing, navigating, and interacting with the content by supplying the page with specific flags that change how their computer interprets the roles, states, and behaviors of elements on the page. ARIA initially began development in 2008 by the W3C. It would see its first widespread adoption and recommendation in 2014. As of now nearly 80% of websites use ARIA in some form. Accessibility should be mandatory for all.
